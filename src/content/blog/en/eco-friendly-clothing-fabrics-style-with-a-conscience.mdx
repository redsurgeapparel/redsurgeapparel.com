## Understanding Eco-Friendly Clothing Fabrics

### Defining Eco-Friendly Fabrics

Eco-friendly clothing fabrics encompass a diverse range of materials that are produced and processed with minimal harm to the environment. These fabrics prioritize sustainability at every stage of their lifecycle, from cultivation or extraction to manufacturing and disposal. Unlike conventional fabrics, which often rely on chemical-intensive processes and non-renewable resources, eco-friendly fabrics embrace natural, renewable, and biodegradable materials.

Organic cotton stands as a prime example of eco-friendly fabric. Grown without synthetic pesticides or genetically modified organisms (GMOs), organic cotton reduces the environmental impact associated with conventional cotton cultivation. It promotes soil health, conserves water, and protects the well-being of farmers and agricultural communities. Additionally, organic cotton is free from harmful residues, making it safer for consumers and the environment alike.

Innovative fabrics like [TENCEL™](https://www.tencel.com/), derived from sustainably sourced wood pulp, represent another facet of eco-friendly textiles. TENCEL™ production utilizes a closed-loop process, where solvents are recycled and reused, minimizing waste and pollution. The resulting fabric is not only soft and breathable but also biodegradable, offering a greener alternative to traditional synthetic fibers.

By understanding the characteristics and benefits of eco-friendly clothing fabrics, consumers can make informed choices that align with their values and contribute to a more sustainable fashion industry. From organic cotton to TENCEL™ and beyond, these fabrics offer a pathway to greener wardrobes and a healthier planet.

### Criteria for Eco-Friendly Fabrics

As consumers become increasingly aware of the environmental impact of their purchasing decisions, the demand for eco-friendly clothing fabrics continues to rise. But what exactly makes a fabric eco-friendly? Let's delve into the key criteria that define these sustainable materials.

#### 1\. Sustainable Sourcing

At the core of eco-friendly clothing fabrics is the sourcing of raw materials in a sustainable manner. This entails using natural fibers that are grown or harvested with minimal environmental impact. For example, organic cotton is cultivated without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers, reducing soil and water contamination while promoting biodiversity. Fabrics derived from renewable resources like bamboo or hemp also fall under this category, as they require fewer resources and have a lower carbon footprint compared to conventional materials.

#### 2\. Low Environmental Footprint

Another essential aspect of eco-friendly fabrics is their low environmental footprint. This encompasses factors such as water usage, energy consumption, and greenhouse gas emissions throughout the production process. Fabrics that require minimal water and energy inputs, as well as those produced using renewable energy sources, are deemed more eco-friendly. Additionally, processes that minimize pollution and waste generation contribute to a fabric's environmental credentials.

#### 3\. Biodegradability and Recyclability

The end-of-life disposal of clothing fabrics also plays a significant role in determining their eco-friendliness. Fabrics that are biodegradable, meaning they can naturally decompose into non-toxic elements when discarded, are preferable as they have minimal impact on landfills and ecosystems. Similarly, fabrics that are recyclable or can be repurposed into new materials through upcycling initiatives contribute to a circular economy, reducing the demand for virgin resources and minimizing waste.

#### 4\. Social Responsibility

In addition to environmental considerations, eco-friendly clothing fabrics should also uphold principles of social responsibility. This involves ensuring fair labor practices, safe working conditions, and fair wages throughout the supply chain. Fabrics that are certified by reputable organizations such as Fair Trade or adhere to internationally recognized labor standards help promote social equity and support the well-being of garment workers.

#### 5\. Transparency and Certification

Lastly, transparency and certification play a crucial role in verifying the eco-friendly claims of clothing fabrics. Look for fabrics that are certified by recognized eco-labels such as GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard), OEKO-TEX, or USDA Organic. These certifications provide assurance that the fabric meets specific environmental and social criteria and has undergone rigorous testing and auditing processes.

By considering these criteria when selecting clothing fabrics, we can make more informed choices that align with our values and contribute to a more sustainable fashion industry. Whether it's opting for organic cotton shirts, bamboo leggings, or TENCEL™ dresses, each decision to embrace eco-friendly clothing fabrics brings us one step closer to a more sustainable wardrobe and a healthier planet.

## Organic Cotton: A Staple of Sustainable Fashion

### What is Organic Cotton?

Organic cotton is not just a fabric; it's a testament to sustainable agriculture practices and a commitment to preserving the environment. Unlike conventional cotton, which relies heavily on chemical pesticides and fertilizers, organic cotton is grown using natural methods that prioritize soil health and biodiversity.

The cultivation of organic cotton begins with the selection of non-genetically modified seeds and the implementation of crop rotation techniques to maintain soil fertility and prevent pest infestations. Farmers refrain from using synthetic pesticides and fertilizers, opting instead for organic alternatives such as neem oil and compost. Additionally, organic cotton farming emphasizes water conservation through techniques like rainwater harvesting and drip irrigation, reducing the strain on local water resources.

By eschewing harmful chemicals and embracing natural cultivation methods, organic cotton production promotes the health of ecosystems and minimizes the environmental impact associated with conventional cotton farming. It also prioritizes the well-being of farmers and agricultural communities by reducing their exposure to toxic substances and ensuring fair wages and working conditions.

### Benefits of Organic Cotton

The benefits of organic cotton extend far beyond its environmental and social impact. From a consumer standpoint, organic cotton offers several advantages that make it a preferred choice for eco-conscious shoppers.

First and foremost, organic cotton is free from harmful residues commonly found in conventional cotton fabrics, making it safer for individuals with sensitive skin or allergies. Its breathable and hypoallergenic properties ensure comfort and peace of mind, especially for those prone to skin irritations or respiratory issues.

Furthermore, organic cotton is known for its durability and longevity, outlasting many synthetic counterparts and retaining its quality wash after wash. Its natural fibers are soft and gentle against the skin, providing a luxurious feel without compromising on sustainability.

From a fashion perspective, organic cotton is versatile and adaptable, lending itself to a wide range of clothing styles and designs. Whether it's a crisp button-down shirt, a cozy sweater, or a breezy summer dress, organic cotton fabrics offer timeless elegance and effortless style for every occasion.

### Challenges and Considerations

Despite its many benefits, organic cotton production is not without its challenges and considerations. One of the primary challenges faced by organic cotton farmers is the transition period from conventional to organic farming practices. During this transition, farmers may experience lower yields and increased labor costs as they adapt to new cultivation methods and manage pest control without chemical inputs.

Additionally, organic cotton farming requires careful management of resources and may be more labor-intensive compared to conventional practices. Farmers must invest time and effort in crop rotation, soil enrichment, and pest management strategies to maintain productivity and ensure the long-term viability of their farms.

Another consideration is the higher price point associated with organic cotton products. Due to the additional costs involved in organic certification, sustainable farming practices, and fair labor standards, organic cotton clothing may be priced slightly higher than its conventional counterparts. However, many consumers are willing to pay a premium for the peace of mind and ethical reassurance that organic cotton provides.

In conclusion, organic cotton stands as a beacon of sustainability in the fashion industry, offering a holistic approach to clothing production that prioritizes environmental stewardship and social responsibility. While it may present challenges and considerations, the benefits of organic cotton – from its superior quality and comfort to its positive impact on farmers and ecosystems – make it a worthy investment for those seeking to embrace eco-friendly clothing fabrics and make a positive difference in the world.

## Hemp: The Versatile and Eco-Friendly Fiber

### Introduction to Hemp Fabric

Hemp fabric is gaining recognition as a sustainable alternative in the world of fashion, offering a plethora of benefits for both consumers and the planet. Derived from the hemp plant, Cannabis sativa, hemp fabric has a long history dating back thousands of years and has recently experienced a resurgence in popularity due to its eco-friendly properties.

Hemp cultivation requires minimal water and no pesticides or herbicides, making it one of the most environmentally friendly crops to grow. Its deep roots help prevent soil erosion and improve soil health, while its fast growth rate allows for multiple harvests in a single growing season. Additionally, hemp requires less land and resources compared to conventional crops like cotton, making it a more sustainable option for clothing production.

### Environmental Benefits of Hemp

The environmental benefits of hemp fabric are manifold and extend beyond its cultivation to its production and end-of-life disposal. Hemp fabric is naturally biodegradable and compostable, meaning it can break down into organic matter without releasing harmful toxins into the environment. This makes it an ideal choice for eco-conscious consumers looking to reduce their environmental footprint.

Furthermore, hemp fabric has excellent durability and longevity, outlasting many other natural fibers like cotton and linen. Its fibers are naturally resistant to mold, mildew, and UV radiation, making it an ideal choice for outdoor and activewear. Additionally, hemp fabric becomes softer and more comfortable with each wash, ensuring lasting comfort and performance.

### Hemp Fabric Applications

Hemp fabric's versatility extends to a wide range of applications in the fashion industry. From clothing and accessories to home textiles and upholstery, hemp fabric can be used in various ways to create stylish and sustainable products.

In clothing, hemp fabric offers a unique blend of breathability, moisture-wicking properties, and durability, making it suitable for a wide range of garments. From lightweight summer dresses and shirts to rugged jeans and outerwear, hemp fabric can be tailored to suit different styles and preferences. Its natural antimicrobial properties also make it an excellent choice for activewear and undergarments, keeping odors at bay during intense workouts or outdoor adventures.

Beyond clothing, hemp fabric is also used in accessories such as bags, hats, and shoes, adding a touch of eco-friendly style to everyday essentials. Its strength and durability make it an ideal material for bags and luggage, offering both functionality and sustainability. In home textiles, hemp fabric can be used for bedding, curtains, and upholstery, providing natural beauty and comfort to living spaces while reducing environmental impact.

Hemp fabric stands as a versatile and eco-friendly option for those seeking sustainable alternatives in the fashion industry. Its environmental benefits, durability, and versatility make it a compelling choice for consumers looking to make a positive impact on the planet without compromising on style or performance. By embracing hemp fabric, we can support sustainable agriculture practices and reduce our reliance on resource-intensive materials, paving the way for a greener and [more sustainable future](/augmented-reality-for-fashion-unleashing-the-future-of-style-in-2024).

## Bamboo: A Sustainable Alternative with a Luxurious Feel

### Exploring Bamboo Fabric

Bamboo fabric is revolutionizing the world of fashion with its sustainable qualities and luxurious feel. Derived from the bamboo plant, this eco-friendly fabric offers a host of benefits that appeal to both consumers and environmentalists alike.

Bamboo fabric is crafted from the pulp of the bamboo plant using a mechanical or chemical process. The result is a soft and silky textile that rivals the feel of luxurious fabrics like silk and cashmere. Its smooth texture and natural luster make it a popular choice for clothing, bedding, and other textiles.

One of the most appealing characteristics of bamboo fabric is its breathability and moisture-wicking properties. Bamboo fibers have a unique structure that allows them to absorb and evaporate moisture quickly, keeping the wearer cool and comfortable in warm weather. This makes bamboo fabric an excellent choice for activewear, underwear, and sleepwear, where breathability and comfort are paramount.

### Environmental Impact of Bamboo Fabric

Despite its luxurious feel, bamboo fabric is also celebrated for its minimal environmental impact. Bamboo is one of the fastest-growing plants on earth, with some species capable of growing up to three feet in a single day. This rapid growth rate makes bamboo highly renewable and sustainable, requiring less water, pesticides, and fertilizers compared to conventional crops like cotton.

Furthermore, bamboo plants are naturally resistant to pests and diseases, reducing the need for chemical interventions in cultivation. This makes bamboo fabric an ideal choice for eco-conscious consumers looking to minimize their environmental footprint.

However, it's essential to note that while bamboo itself is sustainable, the production process of bamboo fabric can vary. Some methods involve the use of harsh chemicals like sodium hydroxide and carbon disulfide, which can have detrimental effects on both human health and the environment if not properly managed. Therefore, it's essential to look for bamboo fabric that has been produced using eco-friendly and closed-loop manufacturing processes to ensure minimal environmental impact.

### Bamboo Fabric in Fashion

Bamboo fabric's luxurious feel and eco-friendly credentials have made it a favorite among fashion designers and consumers alike. From soft and breathable t-shirts to elegant dresses and loungewear, bamboo fabric lends itself to a wide range of clothing styles and designs.

In addition to its comfort and versatility, bamboo fabric also offers natural antibacterial properties, making it an excellent choice for sensitive skin or allergy sufferers. Its hypoallergenic qualities help prevent irritation and discomfort, making it suitable for those with eczema, psoriasis, or other skin conditions.

Bamboo fabric is also known for its thermal-regulating properties, making it suitable for year-round wear. In hot weather, bamboo fabric helps keep the skin cool and dry by wicking away moisture and allowing for better airflow. In colder temperatures, bamboo fabric provides insulation and warmth, keeping the wearer comfortable and cozy.

Overall, bamboo fabric represents a sustainable alternative in the world of fashion, offering a luxurious feel and a host of eco-friendly benefits. Its softness, breathability, and moisture-wicking properties make it an excellent choice for a wide range of clothing and textile applications. By embracing bamboo fabric, we can support sustainable agriculture practices and reduce our environmental impact while enjoying the comfort and style of this innovative material.

## TENCEL™: Innovating Sustainability in Fashion

### Introduction to TENCEL™

TENCEL™, a revolutionary fabric, is making waves in the fashion industry for its sustainable attributes and versatile applications. Developed by the Austrian company Lenzing AG, TENCEL™ is derived from sustainably sourced wood pulp, typically sourced from eucalyptus trees.

The production process of TENCEL™ involves an environmentally responsible closed-loop system, where solvents used in the manufacturing process are recycled and reused, minimizing waste and reducing environmental impact. This closed-loop system sets TENCEL™ apart from traditional textile manufacturing methods, making it a frontrunner in sustainable fabric innovation.

The resulting fabric is soft, breathable, and gentle on the skin, with a luxurious feel that rivals traditional textiles like cotton and silk. TENCEL™ is also highly versatile, suitable for a wide range of clothing and textile applications, from casual wear to [high-end fashion wholesale](/high-fashion-wholesale-the-ultimate-guide).

### Environmental Advantages of TENCEL™

One of the key advantages of TENCEL™ is its minimal environmental footprint compared to conventional fabrics. The production process of TENCEL™ utilizes sustainable forestry practices and requires significantly less water and land compared to cotton cultivation. Additionally, the closed-loop system used in TENCEL™ production reduces water usage by recycling and reusing solvents, further minimizing environmental impact.

Furthermore, TENCEL™ is produced using a non-toxic solvent called N-methylmorpholine-N-oxide (NMMO), which is safer for workers and the environment compared to the harsh chemicals used in conventional textile manufacturing. This makes TENCEL™ a more eco-friendly choice for environmentally conscious consumers looking to reduce their carbon footprint.

Another environmental advantage of TENCEL™ is its biodegradability. Unlike synthetic fabrics like polyester, which can take hundreds of years to decompose, TENCEL™ is biodegradable and can break down naturally over time, returning to the earth without leaving behind harmful residues or microplastics.

### TENCEL™ in the Fashion Industry

TENCEL™ has gained popularity in the fashion industry for its eco-friendly properties and luxurious feel, with many designers incorporating it into their collections. From casual basics to high-end couture, TENCEL™ offers versatility and sustainability without compromising on style or performance.

In casual wear, TENCEL™ is prized for its softness, breathability, and moisture-wicking properties, making it ideal for everyday essentials like t-shirts, jeans, and loungewear. Its natural sheen and drape add a touch of elegance to casual silhouettes, elevating them from basic to sophisticated.

In high-end fashion, TENCEL™ is celebrated for its luxurious feel and eco-friendly credentials, with designers using it to create statement pieces that are both sustainable and stylish. From flowing dresses to tailored suits, TENCEL™ lends itself to a wide range of design aesthetics, offering endless possibilities for creative expression.

TENCEL™ has also found applications beyond clothing, with many brands using it in home textiles and accessories. From bedding and towels to curtains and upholstery, TENCEL™ offers durability, breathability, and sustainability, making it a popular choice for eco-conscious consumers looking to green their homes.

At the end of the day, TENCEL™ represents a groundbreaking innovation in sustainable fashion, offering a combination of environmental responsibility, comfort, and style. Its closed-loop production process, minimal environmental footprint, and luxurious feel make it a preferred choice for eco-friendly clothing fabrics, setting a new standard for sustainability in the fashion industry.

## Recycled Fabrics: Giving New Life to Old Materials

### Overview of Recycled Fabrics

Recycled fabrics are paving the way for sustainable fashion by repurposing old materials and reducing the need for virgin resources. These fabrics are created through a variety of processes, including recycling post-consumer waste such as plastic bottles, discarded clothing, and textile scraps.

One of the most common types of recycled fabrics is recycled polyester, which is made from recycled plastic bottles. The bottles are cleaned, shredded into small flakes, and then melted down to create polyester fibers. This process helps divert plastic waste from landfills and oceans, giving it a new life as a durable and versatile fabric.

Another popular recycled fabric is recycled cotton, which is made from post-industrial and post-consumer cotton waste. Old clothing and textile scraps are collected, sorted, and processed into new yarns, which are then woven or knit into fabric. This process conserves water and energy compared to conventional cotton production and reduces the need for pesticide-intensive cotton farming.

### Benefits of Recycled Fabrics

Recycled fabrics offer a multitude of benefits for both the environment and consumers. From reducing waste and conserving resources to promoting innovation and circularity, recycled fabrics are reshaping the fashion industry in a more sustainable direction.

One of the primary benefits of recycled fabrics is their ability to reduce waste and minimize environmental impact. By repurposing old materials, recycled fabrics help divert waste from landfills and reduce the need for new raw materials, conserving natural resources and reducing energy consumption.

Recycled fabrics also help mitigate the environmental footprint of clothing production by reducing greenhouse gas emissions and water usage. Compared to virgin materials, recycled fabrics require less energy and water to produce, making them a more environmentally friendly choice for eco-conscious consumers.

Furthermore, recycled fabrics promote innovation and circularity within the fashion industry by encouraging a more sustainable approach to design and production. Designers are increasingly incorporating recycled materials into their collections, creating stylish and eco-friendly clothing options that appeal to a growing market of environmentally conscious consumers.

### Challenges and Innovations

While recycled fabrics offer many benefits, they also present challenges and opportunities for innovation within the fashion industry. One of the main challenges is the availability of high-quality recycled materials. Despite advancements in recycling technology, not all materials can be recycled into high-quality fabrics suitable for clothing production.

Another challenge is the need for better infrastructure and systems for collecting and processing recycled materials. Improving recycling infrastructure and implementing more efficient collection and sorting processes can help increase the availability of recycled fabrics and make them more accessible to designers and manufacturers.

Despite these challenges, there are also many innovations and advancements in recycled fabric technology that are driving progress within the industry. From new recycling techniques to innovative blends and finishes, researchers and manufacturers are constantly exploring new ways to improve the quality and performance of recycled fabrics.

In conclusion, recycled fabrics are playing a crucial role in advancing sustainability in the fashion industry. By repurposing old materials and reducing the need for virgin resources, recycled fabrics offer a more environmentally friendly alternative to conventional fabrics. With continued innovation and investment in recycling technology, recycled fabrics have the potential to revolutionize the way we think about clothing production and consumption, paving the way for a more sustainable future.

## Linen: Timeless Elegance with Eco-Friendly Credentials

### Understanding Linen Fabric

Linen, often regarded as the fabric of luxury and sophistication, has been cherished for centuries for its natural beauty and versatility. Made from the fibers of the flax plant, linen is renowned for its breathable, lightweight, and durable qualities, making it a favorite choice for eco-conscious consumers seeking stylish and sustainable clothing options.

The production process of linen begins with the cultivation of flax, a resilient plant that requires minimal water and pesticides compared to other crops like cotton. Once harvested, the flax fibers are extracted from the plant's stalks through a process called retting, where they are soaked in water to loosen the fibers from the woody core.

The fibers are then spun into yarn and woven into fabric, resulting in a textile that is known for its unique texture and drape. Linen fabric is prized for its natural luster and crispness, lending an air of elegance and sophistication to any garment or home textile.

### Environmental Benefits of Linen

Linen boasts a host of environmental benefits that contribute to its status as an eco-friendly fabric. As a natural fiber, linen is biodegradable and compostable, meaning it can break down naturally without leaving behind harmful residues or contributing to landfill waste. This makes linen a sustainable choice for environmentally conscious consumers looking to reduce their carbon footprint.

Additionally, linen cultivation requires fewer resources compared to other natural fibers like cotton. Flax plants thrive in a variety of climates and soil types, reducing the need for irrigation and chemical inputs. Furthermore, flax plants have a short growing cycle, allowing for more frequent harvests and less environmental impact compared to crops with longer growing seasons.

Another environmental benefit of linen is its durability and longevity. Linen fabric is known for its strength and resilience, with garments and textiles made from linen lasting for years with proper care. This longevity helps reduce the need for frequent replacements, ultimately reducing waste and resource consumption.

### Linen in Contemporary Fashion

Linen's timeless elegance and eco-friendly credentials have earned it a place in contemporary fashion, with designers and consumers alike embracing its natural beauty and sustainability. From breezy summer dresses to tailored suits and casual separates, linen offers versatility and style for every occasion.

In warm weather, linen's breathable and moisture-wicking properties make it an ideal choice for lightweight and comfortable clothing options. Its natural cooling effect helps keep the body comfortable in hot temperatures, making it a favorite fabric for summer essentials like shirts, shorts, and skirts.

In addition to its practical benefits, linen's luxurious texture and drape lend an air of sophistication to any outfit. Its natural creases and wrinkles add character and charm, giving garments a relaxed and effortless look that is both chic and timeless.

Beyond clothing, linen is also prized for its versatility in home textiles and accessories. From bedding and table linens to curtains and upholstery, linen offers durability, beauty, and sustainability for every room in the home.

Overall, linen represents a timeless and sustainable choice for eco-friendly clothing fabrics. Its natural beauty, durability, and environmental benefits make it a favorite among designers and consumers seeking stylish and sustainable alternatives to conventional textiles. By embracing linen, we can enjoy the elegance and luxury of this timeless fabric while reducing our impact on the planet and supporting sustainable fashion practices.

## Upcycled Fabrics: Redefining Fashion with Creativity and Sustainability

### Exploring Upcycled Fabrics

Upcycled fabrics are leading the charge in sustainable fashion, transforming discarded materials into new and innovative textiles with a reduced environmental footprint. Unlike recycling, which involves breaking down materials to create something new, upcycling repurposes existing materials without the need for extensive processing.

Upcycled fabrics can be made from a variety of sources, including old clothing, textile scraps, and even industrial waste. These materials are collected, sorted, and creatively reimagined to create unique and eco-friendly clothing fabrics that breathe new life into discarded materials.

### Advantages of Upcycled Fabrics

One of the primary advantages of upcycled fabrics is their ability to reduce waste and minimize environmental impact. By repurposing materials that would otherwise end up in landfills or incinerators, upcycled fabrics help divert waste from the waste stream and conserve valuable resources.

Additionally, upcycled fabrics require fewer resources to produce compared to virgin materials, further reducing their environmental footprint. By utilizing existing materials, upcycled fabrics help reduce the demand for new raw materials, conserving energy, water, and other natural resources in the process.

Another advantage of upcycled fabrics is their unique and creative aesthetic. Because upcycled fabrics are made from a diverse range of materials, each fabric is inherently one-of-a-kind, with its own history and character. This makes upcycled fabrics a favorite among fashion designers and consumers looking for unique and environmentally conscious clothing options.

### Examples of Upcycling in Fashion

Upcycling has become increasingly prevalent in the fashion industry, with designers and brands incorporating upcycled fabrics into their collections in innovative and creative ways. From high-end couture to streetwear, upcycling offers endless possibilities for creativity and sustainability in fashion.

One example of upcycling in fashion is the use of old denim jeans to create new garments. Denim is a durable and long-lasting fabric that lends itself well to upcycling, with old jeans being repurposed into jackets, skirts, and even accessories like bags and hats. By upcycling denim, designers can breathe new life into discarded materials and create stylish and sustainable clothing options.

Another example of upcycling in fashion is the use of textile scraps to create patchwork garments. Textile scraps are often discarded during the production process, but designers can collect and repurpose these scraps to create unique and eye-catching garments that celebrate the beauty of imperfection. Patchwork garments are not only visually striking but also environmentally friendly, reducing waste and promoting resourcefulness in fashion.

In addition to clothing, upcycled fabrics are also used in accessories like bags, jewelry, and shoes. By upcycling materials like leather scraps, plastic bottles, and even old bicycle tires, designers can create stylish and sustainable accessories that make a positive impact on the planet.

At the end of the day, upcycled fabrics are redefining fashion with creativity and sustainability, offering a unique and environmentally conscious alternative to conventional textiles. By repurposing discarded materials into new and innovative fabrics, upcycled fabrics help reduce waste, conserve resources, and promote creativity in fashion. Embracing upcycled fabrics is not only a stylish choice but also a step towards a more sustainable and ethical fashion industry.
